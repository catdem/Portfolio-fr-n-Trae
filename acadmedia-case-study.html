AcadeMedia

Digital Conversation Cards in School Environments

Project Type: UX Design & Research
Duration: 3 weeks
Role: UX Designer & Researcher
Tools: Figma

AcadeMedia - Digital Conversation cards in school environments (Fictitious case)

Background

On behalf of AcadeMedia, our team was tasked with creating a digital version of their widely used conversation cards. Traditionally available only in paper form, the cards support discussions on sensitive and important topics such as mental health, education, values, and future aspirations. The aim of the project was to make the cards more accessible, adaptable to diverse needs, and usable in both classroom and individual settings.

Problem Statement

We addressed two main challenges:

Key Questions

How can a digital version of AcadeMedia's conversation cards best support teachers working with students in grades 6–9?

How can the design meet students' expectations of modern, interactive, and accessible digital tools while preserving the cards' original intent?

Benchmarking & Research

Previous research highlights that conversation cards help young people articulate feelings and values, fostering safety and inclusion. Studies also indicate a growing expectation for digital tools, especially those that are visually engaging and interactive. However, challenges include ensuring accessibility, protecting privacy, and avoiding pressure on students to respond in certain ways.

Method

Our approach combined literature review, competitive analysis, surveys, and user testing. Surveys with both teachers and students revealed needs for structure, flexibility, and digital integration. We developed a prototype for desktop and iPad, aligned with AcadeMedia's design guidelines. Four user tests were conducted to refine usability, functionality, and overall experience.

Results & Insights

The final prototype resembles familiar digital learning tools (e.g., Kahoot), making adoption easier for students and teachers. Key features include:

Individual & Group Modes

Anonymous participation options for both individual reflection and group discussions

Teacher Control

Teachers have full control over question flow and discussion moderation

Shareable Content

Cards can be shared via QR codes and social media platforms

Responsive Design

Optimized for both desktop and iPad use in classroom environments

Insights revealed that digitalization lowers barriers to use, provides flexibility in how conversations take place, and increases student engagement. Teachers valued structure and moderation features, while students appreciated anonymity and interactive elements.

Conclusion & Next Steps

The project demonstrates that digital conversation cards can extend AcadeMedia's impact by fostering meaningful discussions in a safe and modern format. To strengthen the solution further, we recommend:

Grade-Specific Content

Developing grade-specific decks to match students' maturity levels

Expanded Testing

Expanding user testing with a wider range of teachers, counselors, and students

Accessibility & Privacy

Ensuring WCAG-compliant accessibility and transparent GDPR practices

Teacher Training

Offering teacher training materials (guides or short videos) to support classroom use

With continued development, the digital cards have the potential to become an inclusive and engaging tool that supports value-based education across schools.
