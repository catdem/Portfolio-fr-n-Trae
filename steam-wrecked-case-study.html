Steam Wrecked: Collaborative Game Website Design

Background

In this collaborative project, students from the Programming and Game Artist programs worked alongside us UX students to create a complete game experience. The Game Artist team developed the game itself, programmers implemented the website, and our role as UX designers was to design the site to represent the game and its creators. I collaborated closely with another UX student to deliver the UX design for the project.

Problem Statement

The challenge was to create a functional and visually consistent website in under two weeks. The site needed to include information about the game, introduce the development team, and showcase media material connected to the game. Despite the tight timeline, the website also had to reflect the game's artistic style and provide an intuitive user experience.

Research and Discovery

Our research included reviewing reference websites for other indie and student-led games. We received visual assets from the Game Artist team and drew inspiration from the game's style to ensure a consistent graphic language. While we were given creative freedom, it was essential that the site's layout and atmosphere aligned with the game's unique identity.

Design Process

We began by brainstorming concepts and sketching initial wireframes, testing different structures for how to present information in a clear and engaging way. Frames were iterated and refined collaboratively, with continuous debrief sessions involving the whole team. These feedback loops ensured that the website represented not only the game but also the collective effort of all disciplines involved.

The Solution

The final website consisted of several core sections designed to provide both clarity and visual appeal. Together, these elements formed a cohesive website that reflected the game's style and offered an accessible, engaging experience for visitors.

About the Game

Introduced the storyline and gameplay mechanics, providing visitors with essential context about the game experience.

Meet the Team

Highlighted each discipline with dedicated pages for programming, art, and UX, giving every focus area proper recognition.

Media Gallery

Showcased images, concept art, and promotional materials, providing visitors with a visual entry point to the game world.

Results & Impact

Despite the short timeframe, the project was delivered successfully and met the expectations of both the team and the project scope. The website created a professional platform for presenting the game and its creators, while also strengthening collaboration between different disciplines.

Feedback highlighted that the site captured the tone of the game and offered an intuitive navigation structure, demonstrating the effectiveness of our collaborative approach and rapid iteration process.

Conclusion and Next Steps

The project demonstrated the value of close collaboration and rapid iteration under a strict deadline. Lessons learned included the importance of early alignment on visual identity and efficient communication between disciplines.

Future steps could involve expanding the website with interactive elements, such as gameplay previews or downloadable content, and optimizing it further for long-term use as a portfolio and promotional platform.
