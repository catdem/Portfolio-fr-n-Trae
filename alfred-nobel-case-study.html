Nobel Young Innovators

A Challenge for High School Students

Project Type: UX Research & Strategy
Duration: 3 weeks
Role: UX Researcher & Strategist

Case Study: Alfred Nobel's Björkborn – Engaging a Younger Audience

Background

Alfred Nobel's Björkborn faced the challenge of attracting a younger audience in an era where digital and interactive experiences dominate. The aim of the project was to explore how the museum could spark greater interest in Alfred Nobel and the Nobel Prize through events and interactive initiatives.

Challenge

The museum struggled to reach teenagers aged 13–19, as traditional museum experiences are often perceived as static and uninteresting. Two key challenges were identified: creating attractive activities for this age group and adapting the social media strategy.

Key Questions

How can Alfred Nobel's Björkborn attract a younger audience?

What digital and interactive strategies can effectively engage teenagers while preserving the museum's educational mission?

Market Research

Studies of other museums showed that digital solutions, interactivity, and social media are crucial in engaging young visitors. Examples included games, mobile apps, and competitions that encourage interaction.

Surveys with students and teachers confirmed that practical activities and storytelling make museum visits more engaging, while passive exhibitions are often perceived as boring.

Key Insights

  • Digital solutions and interactivity are essential
  • Games and competitions encourage engagement
  • Practical activities resonate with young audiences
  • Storytelling makes content more accessible

Method

The project was based on three steps:

1

Market Research

Research on museums and youth engagement strategies

2

User Research

Surveys and interviews with students and teachers

3

Ideation

Idea development process focused on interactive concepts

This resulted in a concrete proposal: the competition Nobel Young Innovators, supported by a tailored social media strategy.

Results & Insights

Two main concepts were developed:

Nobel Young Innovators

A competition where high school students develop innovative solutions within science and technology, with the final event hosted at Alfred Nobel's Björkborn. This strengthens the museum's role as a platform for young talent.

Social Media Strategy

A content plan featuring themes such as "Nobel Myths – Fact or Fake?" and "Alfred's Lab", making the Nobel Prize more accessible and relevant for young people.

Survey results and teacher interviews showed positive responses to the competition, and several companies expressed interest in sponsoring the initiative.

Conclusion & Next Steps

To attract young audiences, Alfred Nobel's Björkborn is recommended to:

Invest in Interactive Experiences

Develop a stronger digital presence with interactive elements

Implement Social Media Strategy

Execute the proposed content plan to engage young audiences

Establish Annual Competition

Make Nobel Young Innovators a national initiative

Strengthen School Connections

Develop educational materials linking museum and schools

The next step is to anchor these concepts long-term and create a sustainable model that combines the museum's heritage with young people's need for participation and interactivity.
